The Local-First Manifesto.

Smart-home intelligence belongs inside the house. AURA uses a local LLM to understand commands and control appliances on your network, so everyday automation can stay private, fast, and under your control.

shield_lockThe Privacy Paradox

Most smart-home systems depend on cloud assistants to understand even simple requests. That adds delay, creates internet dependency, and sends household commands outside the home before a light, fan, plug, or appliance can respond.

AURA is built around a different idea: run the language understanding locally, then send clear actions to the smart devices already in the house. The system focuses on command interpretation, device routing, and appliance control without making the cloud the center of the home.

Project Goal
Turn natural language commands into reliable smart-appliance actions using a local LLM running inside the home network.

memory Hardware-Rooted Intelligence

The local LLM is the control layer. It interprets what the user wants, chooses the matching device or scene, and sends commands to appliances through the home automation setup.

Designing for Human Rhythm

Technology should make appliance control easier, not force every action through a different app or switch.

With a local LLM, the user can say what they want in normal language: turn on the bedroom fan, make the room brighter, switch to movie mode, or shut everything down. AURA translates that intent into the correct device commands.
